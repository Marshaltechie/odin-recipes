Burger

Descriptions

Sink your teeth into a juicy, flame-grilled beef patty layered with melted cheddar cheese, crisp lettuce, ripe tomato slices, and caramelized onions, all nestled between two toasted, buttery brioche buns. Each bite delivers the perfect balance of smoky, savory flavor and fresh crunch, enhanced by a touch of creamy mayo, zesty ketchup, and a hint of pickles for tang.

Whether served with golden fries or onion rings, this classic homemade burger is a comfort food icon — simple, satisfying, and packed with irresistible flavor.

Ingredients

  • 4 burger buns
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine ground beef, salt, pepper, and optional seasonings (garlic powder, onion powder, Worcestershire sauce).
  2. Mix gently — don’t overwork the meat or it’ll become tough.
  3. Divide into 4 equal portions and shape each into a patty about ¾ inch thick.
  4. Press a small indentation in the center of each patty with your thumb
  5. Heat a skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat and lightly oil it
  6. Place patties in the pan and cook for 3–4 minutes per side for medium, or until desired doneness.
  7. In the last minute, place a slice of cheese on each patty and cover the pan so it melts perfectly.
  8. Preheat grill to medium-high (about 400°F / 200°C).
  9. Grill patties for 3–5 minutes per side.
  10. Add cheese in the last minute of grilling.
  11. Slice the buns in half and spread a little butter on the inside. Toast them on the pan or grill for 30–60 seconds, until golden brown.
  12. Spread burger sauce
  13. Add lettuce, tomato, and onion slices.
  14. Place the cheesy patty on top. Add pickles
